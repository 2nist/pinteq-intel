People & identity
8 sources

The factual anchors of every investigation. Names, addresses, phone numbers, aliases, associates, and history. Always start here before moving to digital sources.

Michigan ICHAT
Critical$10/search
Michigan's Internet Criminal History Access Tool. Criminal history records for Michigan subjects — convictions, arrests, dispositions. Already referenced in Chuck's Smokeball folder structure. Essential for every witness and subject profile in a Michigan criminal case.
ichat.state.mi.us Criminal history
Start using immediately on every case
MiCOURT — Michigan Courts Online
CriticalFree
Public case lookup for Michigan state courts. Search by name to find civil and criminal case history statewide. Reveals prior civil suits, restraining orders, small claims, traffic, and criminal matters that ICHAT may not capture fully.
courts.michigan.gov Court history
Free and essential — use on every subject
PACER — Federal Courts
Start now$.10/page
Public Access to Court Electronic Records. Federal court filings — bankruptcies, federal criminal cases, civil suits, restraining orders across all federal districts. Create a free account; most searches cost under $1. Essential for financial and federal criminal history.
pacer.gov Federal records
Create account this week
Spokeo / BeenVerified
Freemium$20-40/mo
People aggregators that compile address history, phone numbers, relatives, email addresses, and associates from public records. Fast starting points for subject profiles. Good for confirming basic identity and finding address history. Not as deep as TLO but accessible without a professional license.
spokeo.com Identity & address history
Subscribe when billing justifies it
TLO / LexisNexis Accurint
Later$50-200/mo
Professional-grade people search databases used by PI agencies, law firms, and investigators. Deep address history going back decades, phone records, vehicle registrations, asset searches, business associations, and skip tracing. Requires a professional account — PI license or attorney affiliation helps.
tlo.com Deep professional research
Year 2 — post PI license
Michigan SOS & LARA
FreeCritical
Secretary of State business registrations and LARA professional license lookups. Find every business associated with a subject, their registered agent, officers, and status. Check professional licenses — contractor, medical, legal, real estate. You already know LARA from filing the LLC.
mibusinessregistry.lara.state.mi.us Business & license history
Free and immediately useful
Michigan Voter Registration
Free
Michigan's Qualified Voter File is partially public. Voter registration confirms current address, registration status, and party affiliation. Useful for confirming address when other sources conflict or are outdated. Available through county clerks.
mvic.sos.state.mi.us Address confirmation
Free — add to subject profile workflow
SSDI & Obituary Search
Free
Social Security Death Index via FamilySearch confirms deceased status and last known state. Obituaries via Newspapers.com or local funeral home sites reveal family members, associates, and address history going back decades. Useful for locating relatives of subjects.
familysearch.org Deceased verification · family research

Digital footprint & social media
7 sources

People reveal enormous amounts of information voluntarily in digital spaces. Always document findings with a screenshot, the source URL, and a timestamp before the content changes or disappears.

Maltego Community Edition
Start nowFree CE
The industry standard for relationship and network visualization. Maps connections between people, email addresses, domains, social accounts, and phone numbers as an interactive graph. The Community Edition is genuinely powerful for investigation work. Your primary visualization tool — learn this first.
maltego.com Network & relationship mapping
Download and start learning this week
Sherlock
Start nowFree
Open source username enumeration tool. Given one username, searches 300+ social platforms simultaneously and returns all matching accounts. Run it on any username found during investigation to discover the full extent of a subject's social presence. Runs locally via Python.
github.com/sherlock-project Username enumeration
Install this week — Python required
Wayback Machine
CriticalFree
Internet Archive's historical web snapshots. If a subject deletes a post, changes their bio, or removes a website, Wayback may have captured it. Always check Wayback before concluding something doesn't exist. Also use archive.today to create your own snapshots of evidence before it disappears.
web.archive.org · archive.today Historical digital evidence
Use on every investigation — bookmark both
Epieos / Hunter.io
Freemium
Email-to-identity tools. Epieos takes an email address and finds associated social accounts and identity information. Hunter.io finds email addresses associated with domains and names — useful for finding contact info for witnesses or verifying employment claims.
epieos.com · hunter.io Email intelligence
Free tier sufficient to start
Google Advanced Search (Dorking)
CriticalFree
Advanced search operators that surface what normal search hides. Combining operators finds documents, profiles, and mentions invisible to normal results. See the Google Dorking reference section below for the complete operator list with examples.
google.com/advanced_search Universal — every investigation
Learn operators cold — see reference below
WhatsMyName / Namechk
Free
Cross-platform username checkers that double as account finders. Search a target username across hundreds of platforms and see where it exists. Useful for connecting digital identities across platforms when you have one username but need the full picture.
whatsmyname.app Cross-platform identity
Bookmark and use as needed
ExifTool
FreeLearn early
Reads metadata embedded in photos and documents — GPS coordinates, camera model, creation date, software used, and sometimes device serial numbers. A photo posted to social media may contain GPS coordinates revealing where the subject actually was. Free, runs locally.
exiftool.org Photo & document metadata
Install and learn — high value for evidence photos
Communications & relationship mapping
5 sources

Who communicates with whom, through what channels, and when. Phone records are the primary source — your pipeline already handles CSV parsing. Social data provides the context layer.

Phone record CSV analysis
CriticalIn pipeline
You're already building this. Python + pandas to parse call logs into structured data. Identify communication frequency patterns, find clusters of contact around key dates and incidents, surface undisclosed relationships between parties, and flag burner or unknown numbers for follow-up investigation.
Built into Pinteq pipeline Call pattern analysis
Core pipeline component — already scoped
Gephi
Start nowFree
Free, open source network analysis and visualization used by law enforcement and intelligence analysts worldwide. Feed it structured relationship data from phone records and social sources and it renders explorable network graphs. Works alongside Maltego — Maltego for discovery, Gephi for deep analysis.
gephi.org Network analysis & visualization
Download and learn alongside Maltego
Numverify / NumLookup
Freemium
Phone number intelligence — returns carrier, line type (mobile/landline/VoIP), and geographic area code origin. Useful for validating contact numbers in phone records and flagging burner or VoIP lines that suggest attempts to obscure communication. Run every unknown number through this.
numverify.com · numlookup.com Phone number validation
Free tier is sufficient
Truecaller / CallerID
Freemium
Crowdsourced caller ID database. Reverse lookup unknown phone numbers to find associated names and user-submitted labels. Useful for identifying unknown numbers in phone records that don't appear in other databases. Results are crowdsourced — treat as a lead, not a fact.
truecaller.com Unknown number identification
As needed — corroborate findings

Financial & legal history
6 sources

Reveals assets, financial connections, business associations, and civil legal history that subjects don't volunteer. Often produces the most actionable findings for credibility challenges.

Michigan county property records
FreeCritical
County register of deeds — property ownership, purchase history, mortgages, liens, and transfers. Most Michigan counties have free online search. Reveals current and historical addresses, asset ownership, and financial relationships. Kent County and Muskegon County both have online portals. Start there.
kentcountymi.gov · muskegoncounty.org Assets & address history
Free — bookmark both county portals
OpenCorporates
FreeStart now
Global business entity database. Find every company a subject is or was associated with — as officer, director, registered agent, or owner. Covers US state records and international. Cross-reference with Michigan LARA for local entities. Essential for corporate intelligence and following money.
opencorporates.com Business history & associations
Run on every subject with business history
SEC EDGAR
Free
Public company filings database. For subjects with connections to publicly traded companies — executive compensation, insider trading disclosures, ownership stakes, financial relationships. Less relevant for typical criminal defense but essential for white-collar and corporate investigation.
sec.gov/edgar Corporate & financial disclosure
As needed for corporate cases
UCC Filings Search
Free
Uniform Commercial Code filings reveal secured interests — who has claims against a subject's assets. Searched through Michigan SOS. Reveals business relationships, creditors, and asset encumbrances that don't appear in court records. Useful for understanding financial relationships between parties.
michigan.gov/sos/business-services/ucc Asset encumbrances & creditors
Free — add to financial investigation workflow
ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer
Free
IRS Form 990 filings for nonprofits — reveals executive compensation, board members, financial flows, and organizational relationships. Useful when subjects have nonprofit associations. Also use CitizenAudit.org as an alternative search interface for 990 data.
projects.propublica.org/nonprofits Nonprofit & compensation disclosure
As needed
FEC Campaign Finance
Free
Federal Election Commission public donation records. Any political contribution over $200 is public record — searchable by name, employer, and zip code. Reveals political connections, employer information as of the donation date, and address history going back decades.
fec.gov/data Political connections & employer history
As needed — underused and powerful

Workspace & knowledge management
5 tools

The tools that organize your practice, document your methodology, and make you faster and more rigorous on every case.

Logseq
Install tonightFree
Block-based knowledge management with automatic daily journaling and a powerful graph view. Every entry is dated automatically — perfect for investigation logs. Block references let a finding appear in multiple cases without duplication. Local-first, your data stays on your machine. Preferred over Obsidian for investigation workflow.
logseq.com Investigation notes & knowledge base
Install tonight — your personal intelligence notebook
Hunchly
$130/yrMonth 2
Chrome extension that automatically captures and archives every web page you visit during an investigation — with timestamp, URL, and session context. Creates a defensible record of your online research. Used by professional OSINT investigators and journalists. Worth the investment once you're on active cases.
hunch.ly Web evidence capture & archiving
High value — invest when billing justifies it
Obsidian
Free
Local markdown-based knowledge tool with a powerful graph view showing connections between notes. Strong plugin ecosystem. Good alternative to Logseq — use whichever fits your thinking style better. The graph view is genuinely useful for seeing connections across cases and subjects over time.
obsidian.md Personal knowledge management
Alternative to Logseq — try both and choose
Canarytokens
Free
Generates tracking tokens — URLs, documents, emails — that alert you when someone opens them. Useful for confirming document delivery, knowing when a subject accesses a file, or tracking whether shared materials are being forwarded. Advanced tool — learn when you need it.
canarytokens.org Delivery confirmation & tracking
Advanced — learn when relevant
VM / compartmentalized research environment
Learn earlyFree
Run OSINT research in a separate virtual machine or browser profile with a VPN to prevent your real identity from being exposed during investigation. VirtualBox is free. Kali Linux has OSINT tools pre-installed. This is operational security — protect yourself and your client from counter-investigation.
virtualbox.org · kali.org Operational security
Important — set up a clean research environment

Google dorking — operator reference

Learn these operators cold. Combining them is one of the most powerful free investigation techniques available. Every operator below is immediately applicable to case investigation.

Operator What it does Example
site:domain.com
Limits results to a specific website or domain. Find everything indexed from one site.
site:facebook.com "John Smith"
"exact phrase"
Returns only results containing that exact string. Essential for name searches.
"Jason Allen" "Grand Rapids"
filetype:pdf
Returns only that file type. Finds court documents, reports, PDFs containing a name.
"John Doe" filetype:pdf site:gov
intitle:"keyword"
Returns pages where the keyword appears in the page title. More precise than body text.
intitle:"Jason Allen" Michigan
inurl:keyword
Returns pages where the keyword appears in the URL. Finds profile pages and directories.
inurl:profile "Jason Allen"
-keyword
Excludes results containing that word. Clean up noisy results.
"John Smith" Michigan -linkedin
OR / |
Returns results matching either term. Search multiple variations simultaneously.
"Jason Allen" OR "Jason D Allen"
before:YYYY-MM-DD
Limits results to before a specific date. Find historical information or pre-incident activity.
"Jason Allen" before:2025-12-01
after:YYYY-MM-DD
Limits results to after a specific date. Find post-incident activity and statements.
"Jason Allen" after:2025-12-08
cache:url
Shows Google's cached version of a page. Useful if a page has changed or been deleted recently.
cache:facebook.com/jasonallen
Combining operators
The real power comes from combining multiple operators in one query.
"Jason Allen" site:facebook.com OR site:instagram.com before:2026-01-01
Standard investigation workflow

Every investigation Pinteq runs follows this sequence. Consistency is what makes the output defensible and the process repeatable.

01
Subject intake
Log subject name, known aliases, DOB, last known address, role in case, and investigation objective. Define the specific questions this investigation needs to answer.
Tools: Logseq intake template · Pinteq investigation workspace
02
Identity confirmation
Confirm full legal name, DOB, and current address before researching further. MiCOURT, Michigan SOS, and property records. Establish the correct identity to avoid researching the wrong person.
Tools: MiCOURT · Michigan SOS · county property records · Spokeo
03
Legal & criminal history
ICHAT for Michigan criminal history. MiCOURT for civil case history. PACER for federal records. IDACOR for corrections. Document every finding with case numbers and dates.
Tools: ICHAT · MiCOURT · PACER · IDACOR
04
Digital footprint sweep
Google dork for name + location combinations. Sherlock for username enumeration. Platform-specific social media searches. Document and archive everything found — screenshot with URL and timestamp.
Tools: Google dorking · Sherlock · Wayback Machine · archive.today · ExifTool
05
Relationship mapping
Identify associates from social media, phone records, and court records. Map connections in Maltego. Note relationship type, how documented, and timeline of connection. Flag undisclosed relationships to known parties.
Tools: Maltego · Gephi · phone record CSV analysis · social media search
06
Financial & asset research
Property records for address history and asset ownership. OpenCorporates for business associations. PACER for bankruptcy. UCC filings for creditor relationships. FEC for political donations and employer history.
Tools: County deeds · OpenCorporates · PACER · Michigan SOS UCC · FEC
07
Source documentation
Every finding logged with: source name, access method, access date, URL or access path, and confidence level (confirmed / probable / possible / unverified). This is the chain of custody for your intelligence.
Tools: Logseq source log · Pinteq B4 source table
08
Report production
Compile findings into the appropriate Pinteq report format. Facts separated from assessments. Confidence levels rated. Source table complete. Work product header applied. Delivered as a finished analytical product.
Tools: Pinteq B4 template · Pinteq subject profile template · Smokeball memo
